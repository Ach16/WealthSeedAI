# 2. Customer Account Management Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish procedures governing customer account opening, maintenance, monitoring, modification, and closure.

The institution requires all customers to maintain accurate information and comply with applicable regulatory requirements throughout the account lifecycle.

---

## Customer Responsibilities

Every account holder is responsible for:

### Maintaining Accurate Information

Customers must ensure that personal information remains current and accurate.

This includes:

* Full legal name
* Residential address
* Email address
* Mobile phone number
* Employment information
* Tax identification information

### Updating Changes Promptly

Customers must notify the institution within 30 days of any material changes.

Examples include:

* Change of address
* Name change
* Change of mobile number
* Change of employment status

Failure to update information may result in service interruptions or compliance reviews.

### Credential Protection

Customers are solely responsible for protecting:

* Account numbers
* Debit cards
* Internet banking credentials
* Mobile banking credentials
* OTPs
* Security questions

These credentials must never be shared with third parties.

### Suspicious Activity Reporting

Customers must immediately report:

* Unauthorized transactions
* Credential compromise
* Lost cards
* Fraud attempts
* Identity theft concerns

Prompt reporting significantly improves fraud recovery efforts.

---

## Account Status Categories

### Active Account

An account is considered active when customer-initiated transactions occur regularly.

Examples include:

* Deposits
* Withdrawals
* Fund transfers
* Bill payments

### Inactive Account

An account becomes inactive when no customer-initiated transactions occur for 24 consecutive months.

Examples of qualifying transactions include:

* ATM withdrawals
* Online transfers
* Branch transactions

System-generated interest credits do not qualify as customer activity.

### Dormant Account

Inactive accounts may be classified as dormant following regulatory review.

Dormant accounts may experience:

* Transaction restrictions
* Enhanced verification requirements
* Temporary service suspension

---

## Dormant Account Reactivation Procedure

To reactivate a dormant account, customers must:

### Step 1

Visit an authorized branch.

### Step 2

Provide valid identity documentation.

### Step 3

Complete re-verification requirements.

### Step 4

Submit a reactivation request form.

### Step 5

Await approval and account restoration.

Typical processing time:

2–5 business days.

---

## Joint Accounts

Joint account holders share responsibility for account management.

Types of joint account operation include:

### Either or Survivor

Either account holder may operate the account independently.

### Jointly

All account holders must authorize transactions.

### Former or Survivor

The primary holder operates the account while both parties are living.

Specific operational rules are determined during account opening.

---

## Account Closure Policy

Customers may request account closure by visiting a branch.

Requirements include:

* Valid identity proof.
* Account closure request form.
* Settlement of outstanding obligations.
* Return of unused cheque books and debit cards where applicable.

Before closure:

* Pending transactions must be completed.
* Loan obligations must be cleared.
* Negative balances must be settled.

Processing Time:

Up to 3 business days.

---

## Regulatory Compliance Requirements

The institution reserves the right to:

* Restrict accounts.
* Suspend transactions.
* Request additional documentation.
* Freeze accounts when legally required.

Such actions may occur in response to:

* Regulatory directives.
* Court orders.
* Fraud investigations.
* AML compliance reviews.
* KYC deficiencies.

Customer notification will be provided whenever legally permissible.

## Interest Calculation Methodology

Interest on savings accounts is calculated daily based on the closing balance maintained in the account.

The accumulated interest is credited to customer accounts on a quarterly basis.

Example:

If a customer maintains an average balance of ₹1,00,000 and the annual interest rate is 4%, interest accrues daily and is credited every quarter.

This approach ensures fair compensation based on actual account balances.
